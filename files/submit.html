Submit Your Solution

Deadline: February 1, 2026

Submission Guidelines

The Challenge: How can we redesign rural healthcare systems to prevent hospital closures and improve access to quality care for rural Americans?

What We're Looking For

We want innovative, actionable solutions that address the root causes of rural hospital closures. Your proposal can focus on any aspect of the problem, including but not limited to:

  • Policy Reforms: Changes to Medicare/Medicaid reimbursement, insurance regulations, or healthcare legislation
  • Payment Model Innovations: New approaches to hospital financing, value-based care models, or payment bundling
  • Financial Restructuring: Debt relief strategies, new funding mechanisms, or public-private partnerships
  • Technology Solutions: Telehealth expansion, remote monitoring, AI-assisted diagnostics, or mobile health platforms
  • Care Delivery Models: Micro-hospitals, urgent care networks, mobile clinics, or hub-and-spoke systems
  • Workforce Solutions: Provider recruitment and retention strategies, training programs, or scope of practice changes
  • Transportation & Access: Solutions to help patients reach care more easily
  • Preventive & Community Health: Programs to reduce hospital utilization through better preventive care

Submission Formats

Choose ONE of the following formats:

Written Proposal

Length: 1-3 pages (single-spaced)

  • PDF or Word document
  • 12-point font, standard margins
  • Citations and references (not included in page count)

Video Presentation

Length: 3 minutes maximum

  • MP4 format preferred
  • Can include slides, visuals, or speaking directly to camera
  • Upload to Google Drive or YouTube and share link

Required Components

Your submission (written or video) must include:

  1. Problem Summary: Brief overview of the specific issue you're addressing (e.g., payment inadequacy, workforce shortages, maternity care access)
  2. Proposed Solution: Clear description of your idea and how it works
  3. Evidence & Reasoning: Data, research, case studies, or logical arguments supporting your approach
  4. Expected Impact: Who benefits? How many people or hospitals could this help? What outcomes would improve?
  5. Implementation Roadmap: Key steps for putting your solution into practice (who would need to act, what resources are required, timeline)
  6. Challenges & Limitations: Honest assessment of potential obstacles and how they might be addressed

Evaluation Criteria

Judges will evaluate submissions based on:

  • Innovation — Originality and creativity of the solution
  • Feasibility — Could this realistically be implemented?
  • Impact — Potential to prevent closures and improve access to care
  • Clarity — How well-organized and clearly communicated is your proposal?
  • Evidence — Quality of research, data, and reasoning supporting your idea

Important: Plagiarism will result in immediate disqualification. All sources must be properly cited. Original thinking is required — you can build on existing ideas, but your proposal must offer new insights or approaches.
