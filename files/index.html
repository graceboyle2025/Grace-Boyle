The Crisis

Hospital Closures Accelerating

Over 100 rural hospitals have closed in the past decade, and 700+ more — one-third of all rural hospitals — are at immediate risk of closing in the next few years.

Maternity Care Disappearing

Over 100 rural hospitals have stopped delivering babies since 2021. Less than half of rural hospitals still offer labor and delivery services, forcing pregnant women to travel hours for care.

Communities Left Behind

When rural hospitals close, communities lose emergency rooms, clinics, mental health services, and chronic disease management. This leads to higher mortality rates, economic decline, and population outflow.

The Challenge

How can we redesign rural healthcare systems to prevent hospital closures and improve access to quality care for rural Americans?

We're calling on undergraduate and high school students to propose innovative solutions — policy reforms, payment model redesigns, technological innovations, and new care delivery systems that can prevent closures and restore access to essential services.
